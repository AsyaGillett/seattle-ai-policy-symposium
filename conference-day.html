Conference Day

Bring your policy. Leave with ideas.

Date TBD · Lakeside School · Bliss Hall classrooms

“The goal is not just to talk about AI policy. The goal is to draft something schools can actually use.”

Who Can Attend

Students

Interested high school students from Seattle-area independent schools.

Educators

Teachers, librarians, advisors, and administrators involved in AI policy or classroom expectations.

School Teams

Each school is encouraged to send both students and adults so the conversation is balanced.

Proposed Schedule

Opening

Welcome + Keynote

A speaker in AI and education frames the stakes for schools.

Session 1

Policy Exchange

Schools share what their current policy says, what they like, and what they wish were different.

Session 2

Student + Educator Roundtables

Mixed groups discuss grading, disclosure, equity, academic integrity, and trust.

Session 3

Collaborative Drafting

Participants identify shared principles, unresolved questions, and possible policy language.

Closing

Takeaways

Each school leaves with ideas to bring back, and the planning team begins compiling the shared document.

What to Bring

  • Your school’s current AI policy or classroom rubric
  • Examples of what is working well
  • Examples of what feels confusing, unfair, or outdated
  • Questions your school is still trying to answer

Location

The event would be hosted in classrooms on the third floor of Bliss Hall at Lakeside School, giving students and educators space for smaller discussion groups.
