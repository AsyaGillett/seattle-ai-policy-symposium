Our Mission

Helping schools move from “good enough” AI policies to better, fairer ones.

SAIPS is a proposed student-led conference hosted at Lakeside School that brings together students and educators to share current AI policies, talk about what feels useful or frustrating, and draft shared recommendations for the future.

Why this matters

Schools have been trying to adapt to AI quickly. Some policies feel clear and thoughtful. Others feel too vague, too restrictive, or uneven between classrooms. Students are often the people most affected by these rules, but they are not always invited into the process of improving them.

This symposium gives students and educators a chance to compare notes. The goal is not to shame any school for its policy, but to ask what we can learn from one another.

Core question

What should fair and realistic AI expectations look like when students across our schools are graded, evaluated, and applying to colleges in many of the same ways?

Guided by Lakeside’s competencies and mindsets

Competencies

Cognitive flexibility, collaboration and leadership, communication and listening, introspection and emotional intelligence, resilience, and unstructured problem-solving.

Mindsets

Equity and inclusion, ethical, global, growth and learning, healthy, service, and sustainability.

AI policy is a perfect unstructured problem: it is changing quickly, it affects every discipline, and there is no one obvious answer. Hosting this conference would let Lakeside practice leadership through service by helping nearby schools think through the same questions we are facing.
